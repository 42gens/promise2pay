For Small Claims Court Considerations...

If you're considering pursuing a case in small claims court for a personal loan that's owed to you, the type of attorney you'd typically be looking for is a civil litigation attorney, specifically one who handles debt collection or contract disputes. However, there are a few things to consider:


  • Self-representation: Small claims court is specifically designed for individuals to represent themselves without the need for an attorney. The process is generally simpler and more streamlined than other courts.

  • Monetary Limit: Each state has a different maximum amount you can sue for in small claims court. Be sure the amount owed to you is within the jurisdictional limit.

  • Cost: Hiring an attorney might cost more than the amount you're trying to recover, especially if the loan amount is on the lower end. You'll want to weigh the cost of hiring an attorney against the potential benefit.

  • Documentation: Make sure you have all necessary documentation such as written agreements, promissory notes, or any other evidence that proves the existence and terms of the loan.

  • Consultations: Even if you decide to represent yourself, you may want to consider having a consultation with an attorney to get advice on the specifics of your case. Some attorneys offer free or low-cost initial consultations.

  • Mediation: Some courts require or offer mediation as an alternative to a trial. This can be an informal way to resolve the dispute without going through the formal court process.

  • Collection: Winning in small claims court is just the first step. You will then have to collect the judgment, which can sometimes be challenging. Some attorneys specialize in judgment collection, so if you win and have difficulty collecting, you might consider hiring one.
